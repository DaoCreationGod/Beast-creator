Mexican Burrowing Toad (Rhinophrynus dorsalis) - Rotund, termite specialist.
Purple Frog (Nasikabatrachus sahyadrensis) - Pig-like snout, subterranean.
Congo Caecilian (Herpele squalostoma) - Limbless, annulated body.
Mole Salamander (Ambystoma talpoideum) - Stout-bodied, burrows in mud.
Texas Blind Snake (Leptotyphlops dulcis) - Not an amphibian, but included for analogy.
Spadefoot Toad (Spea spp.) - Keratinized "spades" on hind feet.
Boulenger’s Caecilian (Boulengerula taitana) - Pink, worm-like.
Eastern Spadefoot (Scaphiopus holbrookii) - Vertical pupils, explosive breeder.
Giant Palm Salamander (Bolitoglossa dofleini) - Burrows in leaf litter.
Tusked Frog (Adelotus brevis) - Males with "tusks" for combat.
Burrowing Frog (Heleioporus spp.) - Constructs foam nests underground.
Naked Mole-Rat (Heterocephalus glaber) - Mammal, but included for analogy.
Caucasian Parsley Frog (Pelodytes caucasicus) - Cryptic, burrows in damp soil.
Bornean Flat-Headed Frog (Barbourula kalimantanensis) - Fully aquatic, flattened body.
Indian Purple Frog (Nasikabatrachus sahyadrensis) - Bloated body, short legs.
Marbled Balloon Frog (Uperodon systoma) - Inflates when threatened.
Sharp-Nosed Caecilian (Epicrionops spp.) - Pointed snout, fossorial.
Common Spadefoot (Pelobates fuscus) - Nocturnal, explosive breeding.
Giant Caecilian (Caecilia thompsoni) - Up to 5 feet long, limbless.
Desert Rain Frog (Breviceps macrops) - Bloated, squeaky vocalizations.
Baja California Caecilian (Dermophis mexicanus) - Secretive, moist soil dweller.
Túngara Frog (Engystomops pustulosus) - Burrows to avoid predators.
Günther’s Burrowing Frog (Anhydrophryne rattrayi) - Tiny, moss-dwelling.
Hairy Frog (Trichobatrachus robustus) - "Hairs" (dermal papillae) for oxygen uptake.
Couch’s Spadefoot (Scaphiopus couchii) - Desert-adapted, explosive breeder.
